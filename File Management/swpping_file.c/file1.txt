Engineering is not just about solving equations or writing code—it’s about 
learning how to think, adapt, and persist when things don’t work the first 
time. There will be days when concepts feel impossible and failures seem 
constant, but those moments are actually shaping your problem-solving mindset. 
Every bug you fix, every concept you finally understand after struggling, is 
building your strength as an engineer. Stay consistent, be curious, and 
remember: progress in engineering is not always fast, but it is always 
meaningful if you keep going.
